Manny Pacquiao

Professional Boxer

  • 1978- Manny Pacquiao was born in Kibawe, Bukidnon, Philippines, to Rosalio Pacquiao and Dionisia Dapidran-Pacquiao. He is his parents' fourth child.
  • 1992 - At the age of 14, Pacquiao moved to Manila where he became part of the Philippine national amateur boxing team. He has an amateur record of 60-4.
  • 1995- Pacquiao made his professional debut in a four-round bout in the light flyweight division against Edmund "Enting" Ignacio and won.
  • 1998 - His first major boxing honor came in December 1998, when he won a bout against Chatchai Sasakul of Thailand and earned the 'World Boxing Council' (WBC) flyweight title.
  • 2001 - Manny faced Lehlohonolo Ledwaba for the 'International Boxing Federation' (IBF) junior featherweight title and won by a technical knockout. This was his second major boxing title, which he defended four times.
  • 2003 - In a career-defining fight for the Lineal and The Ring Featherweight championship in Texas, he defeated Marco Antonio through a technical knockout. He defended this title twice.
  • 2004- Manny faced Juan Manuel, the holder of WBA and IBF featherweight titles, in Las Vegas. This bout was scored a draw.
  • 2005- Manny fought the Mexican legend Erik Morales for the WBC international and IBA super featherweight titles in the super featherweight division. He lost the 12-round match, but defeated Morales in a rematch that took place on January 21, 2006, in Las Vegas.
  • 2006- He defended his WBC international title against Oscar Larios and won.
  • 2007- He cleared a high school equivalency exam, which made him eligible for college education.
  • 2008- June 2008, Manny fought for the WBC lightweight title and won the title by defeating David Diaz in the ninth round via knockout. December 6, 2008, he defeated Oscar De La Hoya, the six-division world champion, in the welterweight division. On December 22, 2008, he was awarded the 'Philippine Legion of Honor' with the rank of Officer " for winning four world titles in different weight divisions and for being the bes fighter in the world in his time.
  • 2009- May 2009, He defeated Ricky Hatton and won The Ring's junior welterweight title. November 2009, in a fight termed 'Firepower', Pacquiao defeated Miguel Cotto via technical knockout in the twelfth round at the 'MGM Grand', Las Vegas and won the WBO welterweight title. On November 20, 2009, President Macapagal-Arroyo conferred upon Pacquiao the Order of Sikatuna with the rank of Datu with Gold distinction for winning his seventh weight division world title.
  • 2010- Pacquiao's successful foray into politics happened on May 13, 2010, with him being proclaimed congressman of the Sarangani district.
  • 2012- Manny Pacquiao faced Timothy Bradley to defend his WBO welterweight title in a 12-round. Bradley won the fight.
  • 2013- He ran unopposed for his second term as a congressman.
  • 2014- Manny Pacquiao faced Timothy Bradley again at the 'Grand Garden Arena' in Nevada. This time, Pacquiao won and re-established himself as a strong fighter. Pacquiao became the oldest rookie to be drafted in the 'Philippine Basketball Association' whe he was picked as the 11th overall in the first round of the 2014 PBA draft by 'Kia' basketball team.
  • 2016- The Commission on Elections announced Pacquiao as one of the winners of the Philippine Senate seat.
  • 2018- Pacquiao signed with Al Haymon's 'Premier Boxing Champions' (PBC) promotion in October 2018.
  • 2019- He won the WBA (Super) welterweight title. 'Forbes' magazine ranked Pacquiao as one of the 'Highest-Paid Athletes of the Decade' (ranked 8th). On December 11, 2019, Pacquiao graduated from the 'University of Makati' in Makati City with a bachelor's degree in political science, majoring in local government administration.
